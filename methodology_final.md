# Methodology — DeepThought Target Company Research

## Scope and selection
I chose **Hyderabad** as the operating city because the city has a dense life-sciences and med-tech cluster, including Genome Valley and an active Telangana life-sciences ecosystem. Government and cluster material describes Hyderabad as a major life-sciences hub, with a large concentration of companies and supporting infrastructure. Sources used for this framing included the Telangana life-sciences cluster pages and the RICH Hyderabad cluster pages.

## Segments selected
Primary segments used in the target list:
- **Specialty biotech**
- **Specialty diagnostics & life-science tools**
- **Specialty chemicals / APIs / intermediates**
- **Medical devices / scientific instruments** as adjacent specialty categories where the manufacturing fit was still strong

I intentionally avoided generic pharma, pure services, and distributor-heavy businesses.

## Research process
1. Started from Hyderabad life-sciences and diagnostics manufacturer lists, cluster pages, company directories, and recent industry articles.
2. Filtered out:
   - traders and distributors
   - service-only companies
   - large group subsidiaries where the promoter dynamic is not founder-driven
   - companies with no visible operational activity
3. Verified each inclusion using at least one of:
   - official website
   - company LinkedIn profile
   - government / cluster directory
   - ROC / corporate registry listing
   - recent news, awards, hiring, facility, or product updates
4. Rated each company on the six Federer criteria using a conservative evidence standard:
   - **Strong** only when the evidence was direct and explicit
   - **Moderate** when the company looked promising but the public evidence was incomplete
   - **Weak** only when the criterion was not supported by the available evidence
5. Assigned a revenue band as an approximate screening estimate. Where public revenue was not cleanly verifiable, I used **Unknown** or a conservative estimate based on company scale, employee count, facility footprint, and public activity.

## What I learned
- Hyderabad has unusually strong density for diagnostics, biotech, med-tech, and specialty manufacturing.
- Product companies with founder/scientist-led leadership are much easier to qualify than generic pharma or service firms.
- The most reliable signals of fit were:
  - proprietary or niche product portfolios
  - manufacturing addresses, plants, or GMP/ISO certifications
  - founder bios with scientific credentials
  - recent facility, hiring, or product-launch activity

## Notes and caveats
- This is a screening list, not a final legal or financial diligence file.
- For a few companies, public founder/revenue data was incomplete. Those are marked conservatively as Borderline or Fit, not Strong.
- Revenue bands are estimates and should be checked again before outreach.
